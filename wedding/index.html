FAQ

Good to know

Can foreign nationals legally marry in Japan?+

Yes. Many couples hold a ceremony in Japan and complete the legal marriage either here or at home; we'll explain the options for your nationalities and guide the paperwork, in partnership with the appropriate licensed services. We don't provide legal advice, but we make the process clear and manageable.

What kind of ceremony can we have?+

Shinto shrine, Buddhist temple, traditional Japanese, or a Western-style ceremony in a Japanese setting — symbolic or legal. We arrange it with the venue and officiant, and walk your family through each rite in English.

Is everything handled in English?+

Entirely. Your planner is fluent in English and Japanese and is your single point of contact — for you and for every guest travelling in. Nothing important happens in a language you don't follow.

Can you look after our travelling guests?+

Yes — hotels and ryokan, transfers, a welcome dinner and a few private days around the wedding. Many families turn the wedding into a multi-day reunion in Japan, and we design that journey alongside the ceremony.

How far ahead should we plan, and how do payments work?+

Most couples begin 8–18 months out; the best shrines and seasons book early. After your consultation we send a written proposal and a single, transparent quote. A deposit secures your date, and venue, ceremony and supplier costs are arranged at cost with clear terms in writing.

Tell us about your wedding.

Share your story, your roots and the season you have in mind. We'll reply privately to arrange your consultation — no obligation.
